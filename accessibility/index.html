Our Commitment to Accessibility

ValueAlign is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people of all abilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards to achieve this.

Standards We Follow

ValueAlign strives to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with a wide array of disabilities, including:

  • Visual impairments including blindness, low vision, and color blindness
  • Hearing impairments
  • Motor/mobility limitations
  • Cognitive and neurological disabilities
  • Speech disabilities

The WCAG guidelines are organized around four principles, often referred to as POUR:

  • Perceivable - Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive
  • Operable - User interface components and navigation must be operable
  • Understandable - Information and the operation of the user interface must be understandable
  • Robust - Content must be robust enough to be interpreted reliably by a wide variety of user agents, including assistive technologies

Accessibility Features

ValueAlign has implemented the following features to make our digital content accessible:

Navigation and Structure

  • Keyboard Navigation: All functionality is operable through keyboard interfaces, without requiring specific timing for keystrokes
  • Skip Links: We provide "Skip to main content" links to bypass repetitive navigation
  • Consistent Navigation: Navigation mechanisms appear in the same order across the site
  • Focus Indicators: Visible focus indicators help keyboard users track their position
  • Descriptive Headings: Content is organized using properly nested headings to assist with navigation

Content Readability

  • Text Resizing: Text can be resized up to 200% without loss of content or functionality
  • Color Contrast: All text meets WCAG 2.1 Level AA contrast requirements
  • Non-text Contrast: Visual information required to identify controls meets contrast requirements
  • Color Independence: Color is not used as the only visual means of conveying information
  • Customizable Text: We provide options to adjust text size, spacing, and contrast

Multimedia

  • Text Alternatives: All non-text content has text alternatives
  • Captions: All pre-recorded and live video content includes captions
  • Audio Descriptions: Video content includes audio descriptions for visual elements
  • Transcripts: Audio-only content includes text transcripts
  • Media Controls: Audio/video players have accessible controls and don't auto-play

Assistive Technology Support

  • Screen Readers: Content is compatible with screen readers like JAWS, NVDA, and VoiceOver
  • ARIA Roles: ARIA attributes are used to enhance accessibility where HTML semantics are insufficient
  • Form Labels: All form fields have properly associated labels
  • Error Identification: Form errors are clearly identified and described in text
  • Live Announcements: Dynamic content changes are announced to screen reader users

Accessibility Testing and Known Limitations

Our Testing Process

ValueAlign tests the accessibility of our website and platform using the following methods:

  • Automated Testing: We regularly scan our website using tools like Axe, WAVE, and Lighthouse to identify potential accessibility issues
  • Manual Testing: Our design and development teams perform manual tests using keyboard-only navigation and various screen readers
  • User Testing: We conduct sessions with users who rely on assistive technologies to gain real-world feedback
  • Third-party Audits: We periodically engage accessibility specialists to perform comprehensive audits
  • Continuous Integration: Accessibility checks are built into our development workflow

Known Limitations

Despite our efforts to ensure accessibility, we recognize that there may be limitations or areas that need improvement. Currently identified challenges include:

  • Some dynamic content in interactive assessments may be challenging for screen reader users
  • Certain data visualizations in reports have alternative text descriptions, but may not convey all nuances
  • Legacy content from before our accessibility implementation may not fully meet our current standards
  • Some third-party integrations may have accessibility limitations outside our direct control

We are actively working to address these limitations and improve the accessibility of our platform. If you encounter any accessibility barriers not listed here, please let us know using the feedback mechanisms provided below.

Feedback and Assistance

Reporting Accessibility Issues

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of the ValueAlign platform. If you encounter accessibility barriers or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us through one of the following methods:

  • Email: accessibility@valuealign.com
  • Phone: 1-800-555-1234 (Toll-free, 9am-5pm Pacific Time)
  • Feedback Form: Use our Contact Form and select "Accessibility Feedback" from the dropdown menu

When reporting an issue, please provide:

  • The URL or location where you encountered the issue
  • A description of what you were trying to do
  • The specific barrier or difficulty you experienced
  • Any assistive technology you were using (if applicable)

Accommodation Requests

If you need specific assistance or accommodations to use the ValueAlign platform, we are here to help. Please contact our Customer Support team, and we will work with you to find an appropriate solution.

We aim to respond to all accessibility feedback within 2 business days and to resolve issues as quickly as possible.

We are continuously working to increase the accessibility and usability of our platform and in doing so strive to adhere to many of the available standards and guidelines.
